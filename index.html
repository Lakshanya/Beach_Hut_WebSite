Sri Lanka · East Coast

One family. One coastline. Two ways to slow down.

For over a decade, the same family has kept two stretches of Sri Lanka's east coast open to travellers — one built around the surf and the sound of a busy season, the other built around the quiet of an empty beach. Same values, two different rhythms.

Our history

Two guesthouses, one story

It started with a single beach hut in Arugam Bay, and grew, a few years later, into a second home further down the coast — built for guests who wanted the same welcome, somewhere quieter.

2014 · ARUGAM BAY

Ranga's Beach Hut opens

Founded right on the sand in Sri Lanka's best-known surf town, built to give backpackers and surfers an affordable, homely place to stay — soon recognised by The Guardian, UK as one of the country's best budget stays.

2016 · KOMARI

Lighthouse Beach Hut follows

A second property, 15km south, built with a different pace in mind — only eight cabanas, solar power, and an organic farm, on a wide stretch of coastline near Komari Lagoon.

Our goal

Keep the coast honest

"We wanted two places where the coastline still does most of the talking — not the furniture."

Both properties are run with the same intent: simple, comfortable rooms, real local food, and a location that does the heavy lifting. Whether guests come for the surf season or for the quiet, the goal is the same — a stay that feels like it belongs to this coast, not a copy of one built anywhere else.

Eco approach

Built light, on purpose

Sustainability looks a little different at each property, but the underlying rule is shared: build with what the coast already has, and take as little from it as possible.

Materials

Natural, local building

Lighthouse Beach Hut's cabanas are built mostly from natural materials sourced around the property, keeping construction in step with the surrounding land.

Energy

Solar power

Solar panels supply Lighthouse Beach Hut's power — a quieter footprint for a property that's already built around quiet.

Food

Organic, on-site farming

Meals at Lighthouse are prepared from produce grown on the property's own organic farm, and both kitchens lean on local, home-style cooking over anything shipped in.

Lighthouse Beach Hut's eco-building is the most developed expression of this today — the same thinking guides how Ranga's Beach Hut in Arugam Bay is run and grown over time.
